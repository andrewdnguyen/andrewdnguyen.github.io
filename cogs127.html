Improving Physical Connections on LinkedIn

Project Objectives

In redesigning LinkedIn, our team hopes to enable workers in the tech industry to improve their professional skills and networks.

Design Prompt

Using an well-known social media app, extend or redesign a feature that helps encourage specific types of people in one's surrounding area to get together in-person and physically interact in a meaningful way.

Target User

Our team has decided to use workers in the tech sector as our target user. This demographic was chosen for reasons including but not limited to:

Size of Industry

The tech sector is one of the most rapidly growing industries in the US, employing nearly 12 million people in total, with roughly 261,000 jobs added in the last year. As a group, we expect that this continuous growth will incentivize workers in the industry to pursue further development within this field.

Access to a Broad Range of Target Users

As a large university with reputation both in academia and industry, we have easy access to professionals of various levels of skills from students beginning their careers through internships to alumni with years of experience in the field. UCSD also has several programs which cover a variety of tech career paths from software engineering to data science to UX design and more.

Empathy and Equity

As individuals nearly entering the workforce ourselves, we as a group find it easy to empathize with our target users as we along with many of our peers look to develop ourselves for full-time employment.

We also feel that while UCSD and other large universities provide opportunities for development to their students, many people without access to these opportunities are at a disadvantage when competing for jobs and pay in the industry. By improving a relatively accessible application such as LinkedIn we hope to not only improve the usefulness of the app overall, but also help those with fewer development opportunities.
